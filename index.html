Cryptography and Encryption

What is Cryptography?

  • Greek: kryptos (hidden), graphein (writing)
  • Study of secret communication
    • The message is to be known only by those intended to know about it.
  • Encryption is the application of Cryptography
  • Two major components: Cipher and Algorithm
    • Cipher is the encoded text (which seems garbled to the naked eye) - the scrambled version of the hidden message.
    • Algorithm is the process of decoding the cipher so that one can retrieve the original message.
  • No method of encryption is foolproof: an encryption's best friend is time.

Encryption: Polyalphabetic Cipher

  • The individuals sharing secret information agree on a secret word.
  • The character position of each individual character in that secret word, is used to shift the message you are hiding.
  • Example:
    • Secret word: Snake (Shift of 19,14,1,11,5)
    • Secret message: Imperial March
    • Shift every letter in the message "Imperial March" in the sequence of 19-14-1-11-5
      • Shift I by 19       Shift M by 14       Shift P by 1       Shift E by 11       Shift R by 5       Shift I by 19
      • Notice how we shift by 19 again on the second I of "Imperial".
      • You keep reusing the same sequence of shifts until you reach the end of your message.
  • Weakness: Frequency Analysis

Encryption: The One-time pad

  • The individuals sharing secret information agree on a secret shift sequence.
  • Each letter on the message to be encrypted is shifted by a certain amount.
  • The longer the message to be encrypted and the less repetitive the shift sequence is, the better
  • Shift every letter in the message "Imperial March" randomly
    • Shift I by 1       Shift M by 5       Shift P by 4       Shift E by 6       Shift P by 7      
    • You continue to shift by a random set of numbers that you and the recipient agree on.
  • The strongest form of encryption but impractical in the internet.
  • Notice how each letter of the message has 26 possible combinations. There are 26^n possible combinations for a message of n-length.
